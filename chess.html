The final project I worked on during the FdA Graphic Design course at Camberwell College of Arts, gave me the freedom to create my own brief. Following a great deal of research in to the game of chess, I discovered that all chess literature, and most interactive forms of teaching chess denoted positions and piece movements in the exact same way.

Phrases such as "A knight on the rim is grim" are used to teach players about the optimal placement of certain pieces on the board, but are never represented in a visual way which provides an easy understanding of the concepts. In a six poster series, I attempted to visualise every potential movement that could be made from every legal position in an individual poster for each piece, arranging 64 8x8 diagrams (one for each square) in an 8x8 grid as to reflect the layout of the board.
